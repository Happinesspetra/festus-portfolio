about the artist

My name is FESTUS ABIODUN OGUNLEKE, Born on December 17, I developed an early passion for art and painting. This creative inclination led me to pursue a degree in Fine Arts at Obafemi Awolowo University, where I honed skills and nurtured artistic vision. "For me, painting is a journey into the heart of humanity. I seek to capture the fragile beauty of human experience, with all its complexities and contradictions. Through art, I aim to inspire empathy, spark self-reflection, and celebrate the resilience of the human spirit." My artistic influences span diverse cultural and historical contexts, drawing from African, European, and contemporary art. Inspirations include the works of masters like Damilola Opedun, Odeyemi oluwaseun, Alade ife, Awodiiya Toluwani. learn more

artist statement

My art explores the profound connection between humanity and the natural world. Through intricate root patterns on human skin, I symbolize the inseparable bond between our existence and the earth's vitality. The root motif represents: - Anchorage: Our deep-seated connection to the land and its rhythms - Nourishment: The earth's sustenance for our physical and spiritual growth - Resilience: The capacity to thrive amidst adversity, like roots adapting to the soil. To convey the fluidity of human experiences, I incorporate: - Ripple textures: Evoking the concentric circles that spread from a single action, illustrating the far-reaching impact of our choices - Circle mirrors: Reflecting the multifaceted nature of human emotions, inviting self-contemplation and introspection. My work poses questions: - How do we root ourselves in the earth's wisdom? - How do our experiences ripple out, influencing the world around us? - What reflections reveal our true selves? Through this visual language, I aim to: Reawaken a sense of unity with the natural world, Inspire self-reflection and personal growth Celebrate the beauty of human resilience. Mediums: Mixed media, acrylic, and oil paint. Influences: African diasporic traditions, ecological art, and philosophical texts on human-nature interconnectedness.
